Recipe for Pasta

Description

Masala Pasta is an Indian-Italian fusion dish that mayhave originated on the streets of India, selling it as street food. Not a traditional dish but it has been a favorite & made in almost every Indian home, especially with kids

Ingredients

  • 1 and a half cupsof Penne pasta
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • Carrots, peas, bell peppers, onions, garlic and other veggies
  • Tomatoes
  • Half a teaspoon of garam masala
  • Half a teaspoon of Kashmiri Red chilli powder
  • 1 tablespoon Kasuri methi
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon of tomato ketchup

Steps

  1. Fine chop 1 medium onion, 2 medium tomatoes(1 and a quarter cup deseeded and chopped or 1 cup pureed). Grate or mince 2 to 3 garlic cloves and half an inch ginger to make 2 teaspoons of ginger garlic
  2. Peel and chop 1 medium carrot and a quarter cup of bell peppers.
  3. Bring 6 cups of water to a boil in a large pot. When the water begins to boil add 1 teaspoon salt, 1 and a half cups of Penne pasta and 1 teaspoon of oil
  4. Cook the pasta al dente on a medium flame. Pasta should be soft yet cooked but not chewy. Refer the package of pasta for exact cook time as each brand has a different cook time. Roughly boil for 8-9 minutes
  5. Once done drain the pasta to a colander. Save three and a quarters cup pasta cooked water before draining. You can use thistowards the end to bring the pasta to consistency.
  6. Heat 1 and a half tablespoons oil and 1 and a half tablespoons butter in a pan . Add half a teaspoon cumin seeds. When the seeds begin to sizzle, add garlic and ginger. Fry for a minute
  7. Add the chopped onions and fry until they turn golden
  8. Add the chopped vegetables and fry for 2-3 minutes
  9. Stir in the salt and spices-red chilli powder, garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric. If you want you can also add cumin and fennel powder
  10. Add tomatoes and tomato ketchup
  11. Mix and cook on a medium-high heat until the masala begins to smell good or release oil. Make sure the smell of uncooked tomatoes is gone and your veggies are cooked through. If not, reduce the heat and cook on alow heat until the veggies are tender
  12. Pour a quarter cup water.
  13. Cook until the masala becomes slightly thick, aromatic and saucy. Do not dry out the masala completely at this stage. Add the kasuri methi
  14. Add the cooked pasta and mix well. If the masala is dry, splash more water. Taste testand add more salt if needed. Cover and cook on a low heat until the pasta becomes hot, for about 2 minutes
  15. Sprinkle Coriander leaves and serve masala pasta hot
